Project
description

Reaching inaccessible locations for news coverage, especially in cases of natural disasters and accidents, in a timely and cost-efficient manner is crucial for good reporting for news agencies. Currently, one of the most common ways to collect video evidence for such events is through the usage of helicopters. However, this comes at a great cost, which is many times unbearable, especially for smaller news agencies. DRONEWS aims to address this market gap by developing an end-to-end crowdsourcing platform enabling News Agencies to source in a cost-effective manner, near-real-time, user generated drone video streams to enrich news coverage and journalistic content towards increasing their monetization potential. The aim of the DRONEWS project is to provide news agencies and digital media companies with the ability to source near real-time user generated drone videos of remote locations and/or urgent events such as accidents, disasters etc. The key innovation of DRONEWS is that it enables the crowdsourcing of drone-captured videos in real-time, the verification of the uploaded videos, video intelligence for key frame extraction and annotation of captured scenes, and the automatic removal of sensitive information from these videos.

Aims

DRONEWS addresses the modern digital and creative sector and particularly focuses on the Media vertical industry in which Drone technology brings significant disruption and opens up unprecedented opportunities to address the increasing needs for video content as a primary means of communication with all types of audiences. The opportunity to leverage the high and ever-increasing penetration of drones for crowdsourcing videos from areas of interest in order to enrich digital news and articles has so far not been possible due to the lack of an appropriate drone video crowdsourcing platform. DRONEWS addresses this gap by supporting the needs of news agencies and providing incentives to non-professional drone owners to use their drones to collect videos from a nearby area where for example a disaster or other news-worthy incidence is taking place or simply for offering to the audience fresh current videos of the areas mentioned in the article (depending on viewing day/time). Our ambition is for DRONEWS to become a go-to option for aerial footage collection for news and digital content generation agencies and enable a sharing economy paradigm directly benefiting drone owners as well.

Social/Environmental aspects

From a social perspective, DRONEWS enables good journalism practices by providing footage that is compliant with ethics and privacy protection standards. The privacy by design principle on which DRONEWS is based provides a solution to both big and smaller news agencies for provisioning privacy compliant video content. Furthermore, the reduced costs and time and the lower technical barriers for drone footage collection enabled by DRONEWS especially for disaster incidences allows faster incidence coverage with huge potential (possibly life-saving) benefits for locating and helping those impacted the most (women, children, the elderly). From an environmental point of view, DRONEWS can contribute in reducing GHG emissions for collecting both aerial and land footage. The pollutants produced when dispatching helicopters with camera crews come from the combustion of aviation fuel. A typical 1 hour trip with a conventional helicopter for aerial video recordings is emitting around 150-300Kg of CO2. We estimate that DRONEWS can save up to 1Mn tones of CO2 emissions per year if used to substitute the transportation of camera equipment and aerial footage operations. Furthermore, helicopters are producing high levels of noise with 100 dB at 30 meters above the ground which is 8 times as loud as vacuum cleaner. Such noise can disrupt animal habitats and create significant health damage after a prolonged exposure.

Partners

The DRONEWS project will be executed as partnership between 4 innovative SMEs (3 in Greece, 1 in Romania) with capacities and know-how to deliver the services envisioned in DRONEWS. The partners are complementing each other in terms of market and application leadership (MEDIA2DAY) and technology expertise (HYPERTECH, Pragma-IoT, Axiologic). HYPERTECH’s team has extensive experience in complex software development projects and international collaborative R&D projects and it will be in charge of overall project management, will lead the DRONEWS architectural specifications and implementation of the platform back-end and app, as well as contribute to testing/validation. The main team of MEDIA2DAY, having 15 years of experience in digital publications, journalism, news coverage, as well as in the implementation of large IT projects involving the creation and marketing of related services, will support the requirements analysis and lead the dissemination and validation activities on the Euro2day.gr and In2Life.gr online news portals which have an active online community of thousands of users and freelance reporters. Furthermore, Pragma-IoT is a spin-off company of the largest research center in Greece, namely Center for Research and Technology Hellas, with several key products (such as HUBA – Human detection & tracking in retail stores, public service buildings, airports, etc) which have been awarded, while the company was also one of the awarded companies in the European Data Incubator for providing solutions related to big data management.The same stands for Axiologic partner, which offers privacy-enhancing tools for data management and storage
